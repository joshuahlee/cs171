2. Infographic Design Critique

The audience are those who might be interested in working at Google or are interested in benchmarking salaries to Google or between different positions at the firm. The question that the infographic answers are "How much do different positions at Google make?". The data is mainly categorical and quantitative. Breaking that down more specifically, the categorical data are the position types where as the numerical quantitative data is split up between a lower bound and a higher bound to produce a range of expected income. The data also ranks each position by the lower bound going from highest being 1 to the lowest being 10. The data is visually encoded by color coding the positions to the income range along with a corresponding slice of the middle graph. A specific thing that I like about this visual is that it is clear which positions make how much money. The one thing that can be clarified is what the corresponding slice in the middle represents. There are no legends that describe what the center circle should be interpreted as, and because the slices are over lapping and don't make a whole it makes it confusing to understand the significance of the slices in relationship to other slices and the whole graph. My assumption of what the center part represents is that the area corresponds to how much money goes to paying those specific positions in relation to others however that is not made very clear.
